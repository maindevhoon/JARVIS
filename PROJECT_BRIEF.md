# Jarvis — A Memory Butler for Your Digital Life

## The idea

Jarvis is a private, local-first AI assistant that understands what is happening across your computer. It does not live inside Slack, Discord, Zoom, or any single application. With the user's permission, it observes meetings through screen and audio capture, remembers important context, and helps proactively.

Think of it as **Jarvis from Iron Man or Alfred from Batman**: a personal digital butler that watches your work unfold, organizes what matters, and prepares useful work before you ask for it.

## What it does

- Captures user-authorized meetings from the screen, system audio, and microphone.
- Understands discussions, decisions, deadlines, people, projects, and action items.
- Builds an evolving memory of the user's work using **Supermemory Local**.
- Presents concise cards for meeting summaries, commitments, changes, and unresolved questions.
- Links every important memory back to its timestamped transcript or screen evidence.
- Notices requests made during meetings and can start useful background work automatically.

For example, if someone asks the user to present a pitch deck or investigate whether an idea is feasible, Jarvis can recognize the request, create a background research task, and prepare the findings. When the user returns, Jarvis can say:

> “You were asked to evaluate the idea during the product meeting. I researched the market, technical feasibility, and key risks. Your brief is ready.”

## Why Supermemory

Supermemory is not just used to store transcripts. It becomes Jarvis's long-term brain: connecting people, projects, decisions, and commitments across many sessions while understanding when information changes.

This lets Jarvis answer questions such as:

- What happened in my meetings today?
- What did I promise to deliver?
- Which deadlines or decisions changed this week?
- What work did you complete for me in the background?
- Prepare me for my next conversation about Project Nova.

The system runs with **Supermemory Local**, keeping sensitive workday context on the user's machine.

## Hackathon demo

1. A short meeting asks the user to research and present the feasibility of a product idea.
2. Jarvis captures the request, extracts the action item, and adds it to Supermemory.
3. Jarvis autonomously starts a background research task.
4. A later meeting changes the deadline or project owner, and Jarvis updates its understanding.
5. The user opens the Jarvis interface and receives cards showing:
   - what happened;
   - what changed;
   - what requires attention;
   - what Jarvis already completed;
   - the evidence behind every conclusion.

## One-line pitch

> **Your computer sees your workday. Supermemory makes it remember. Jarvis turns that memory into action.**

Jarvis is not another meeting bot. It is an intelligent, proactive memory layer for the user's entire digital work life.
